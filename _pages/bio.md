I was born and raised in Macau. 
After graduating from Pui Ching Middle School in Macau, 
    I moved to Hong Kong and studied theoretical physics at the Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK).
I developed my interests in theoretical astrophysics during my undergraduate studies,
    but decided to expand my scope by conducting research in 
    observational astronomy at the University of Hong Kong (HKU)
    during my MPhil studies.
During my PhD, I focused on modelling the neutrino and gamma-ray emission from the Milky Way Centre.
I am now a postdoctoral researcher at RWTH Aachen University,
    studying the cosmic-ray and gas distributions in our Galaxy.
In my free time, I like to play basketball and enjoy music.
